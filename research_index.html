Research

Computational Neuroscience

Human auditory perceptual switching study

Understanding how perception arises from a sensory stimulus is a challenge in neuroscience. In this study, we analyzed human electrocorticographic recordings obtained during an auditory task, ran a battery of test and built a machine learning pipeline to predict perceptual behavior. Our research identified signatures of subjective perception and laid the foundation for further building dynamic models that capture the temporal evolution across multiple cortical areas.

Computational Biomodeling

Predictive modeling of cartilage degeneration

Our research focused on building a series of multi-scale mathematical models to investigate the relationship between cell death and the inflammatory process for cartilage degeneration. We proposed three models under different scenarios of injuries, simulated the models, and validated the results with the experimental data. Our research provides helpful information which can not be easily obtained in biological experiments, and eventually lead to biomarker discovery and potential therapies.

Human Auditory Perception


Rabbit or a duck? It is a famous ambiguous figure in which the brain switches between seeing a rabbit and a duck. Same as the visual illusion, bistable auditory perception is a phenomenon in which listeners experience switching between percepts even listening to the same sound track. ABA tone triplets can elicit bistable auditory percepts. In an ABA_ tone sequence, listeners' percepts switches between:
  • One stream (integration) → integrated auditory object ABA−ABA− . . ..
  • Two streams (segregation) → two concurrent sounds A−A−A−A− . . . and −B−−−B−− . . .
Try it yourself and see whether your percept switches or not.
Our study utilized electrocorticography (ECoG), which offers a unique opportunity to study auditory cortical processing in humans with high spatial and temporal resolution. ECoG activity was recorded during an auditory streaming paradigm, including stimuli that support auditory perceptual bistability, to characterize the transformation of acoustic stimulus attributes into auditory percepts. We report statistically significant differences in average evoked potential (AEP) associated with 1-stream and 2-stream percepts, in core and non-core auditory cortex, surrounding auditory-related temporoparietal cortex, and frontal areas. In addition, we developed classifiers to identify spatial maps of percept-related differences in the AEP, corroborating findings from statistical analysis.

Cartilage Degeneration Modeling


Traumatic injuries of articular cartilage result in the formation of a cartilage lesion and contribute to cartilage degeneration and the risk of osteoarthritis. A better understanding of the framework of a cartilage lesion formation would be helpful in therapy development. Toward this end, we present multi-scale computational models to describe the articular cartilage lesion formation under two different senerios (intensive cyclic loading and a single blunt impact). Our models focus on the “balancing act” between pro- and anti-inflammatory cytokines which can lead to biomarker discovery for early diagnosis of osteoarthritis.
Next step, we built an effective and robust computational toolkit to numerically solve our models. The simulation results show a successful capture of chondrocyte behavior and chemical activities associated with the cartilage lesion which is also valided by experimental results. We anticipate that our in silico models of cartilage damage can be used to provide information that may not be easily obtained through in in vivo or in vitro studies.
